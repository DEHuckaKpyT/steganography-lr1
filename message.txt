Glacier National Park is located in the Rocky Mountains, on the territory of the U.S. state of Montana, on the border with the Canadian provinces of Alberta and British Columbia. The area of the park is 4101 kmÂ².

Glacier Park has more than 130 lakes, the largest of which is Lake McDonald.

There are 1132 species of plants growing on the territory of the national park. There are also 62 species of mammals, 260 species of birds, 6 species of amphibians, 3 species of reptiles and 23 species of freshwater fish.
